Corporate Censorship in the Digital Age

Dec 27th, 2011

Measuring Freedom

While I’ll leave it to historians and politicians to debate the validity of this statement, IMO, a country’s freedom can be understood by guestimating the answers two simple questions:

  1. How freely the citizens are allowed to travel – both within their country as well as in-and-out of their country.
  2. How freely the government allows information to be dispersed.

While this posting only focuses on freedom of information, I mention the above because I feel that it is important to keep in mind how critical freedom of information is.

When free information flow is impacted, it does not matter whether it is censored by a totalitarian government to control the thoughts of its people or a search provider to stay out of trouble legal troubles (see Wikipedia’s article on Google Censorship and recent statistics about Google’s censoring)1.
